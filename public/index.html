    📜 How to Play

    The Game

    NightFall is a social deduction game. Players are secretly assigned roles and unique game identities ("The Rose", "The Raven", etc). Each night, killers strike and everyone investigates. Clues reference identities — deduce who the killer is!

    🎮 QTE Kill System

    Killers don't just click to kill — they must complete a Quick Time Event (press keys in sequence). A perfect QTE = clean kill, no clues. A failed QTE = evidence left behind pointing to the killer's game identity.

    ⚠ The QTE gets harder with each kill — serial killers get sloppy!

    🔍 Investigation System

    Every alive player can investigate a suspect at night via QTE. Better accuracy = stronger clues.

    Detectives get an easier QTE — they're trained investigators. Civilians can try too, but it's harder.

    Core Roles

    🗡
    Killer
    Choose a victim, then complete a QTE to attack. Sloppy kills leave evidence at the crime scene.
    🔍
    Detective
    Investigate with an easier QTE. Your training gives you stronger, clearer results than civilians.
    🧑
    Civilian
    Can investigate at night (harder QTE). Observe, discuss, and vote wisely — every clue matters.

    Optional Roles

    🩺
    Doctor
    Protect one player per night, then investigate. Cannot self-protect two nights in a row.
    🤡
    Jester
    Wins if voted out. An agent of chaos — the game continues for others.

    Game Flow

    🌙 1. LIGHTS OUT: The manor goes dark. Killer selects a victim → QTE attack. Doctor protects (if enabled).

    🔦 2. LIGHTS ON: Murder discovered + crime scene evidence. All players investigate a suspect via QTE. No talking!

    🍽 3. DINNER: Everyone gathers at the table. Discuss clues, share findings, and vote to execute a suspect.

    ⚖ 4. VERDICT: Most-voted player is executed. Their role is revealed: Killer ☠, Innocent 😇, or Jester 🤡.

    🔄 Repeat until one side wins.

    ⚠ False Evidence

    Low QTE accuracy doesn't just mean weak clues — it can produce FALSE evidence pointing to innocent players. The accuracy warning (🟢🟡🔴) tells you how reliable a clue is. Don't blindly trust red-flagged evidence!
